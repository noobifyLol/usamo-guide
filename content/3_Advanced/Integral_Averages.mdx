## Overview

How do you take the average of $0.5x^2 + 1$ from $3$ to $8$? Well, we can use something called **integral averages**!

## 1. Introduction

An integral average is a method of taking the average of a function across a range by using integrals. It relies on the following formula:

$$
\text{average} = \frac{\displaystyle\int_{\text{lower bound}}^{\text{upper bound}} f(x)\,dx}{\text{upper bound} - \text{lower bound}}
$$

This simply finds the area under the relevant portion of the curve and divides it by the length — to find the height it would be if the shape were a rectangle, which is the average height and thus the average value.

## 2. Worked Example

Applying the formula to the problem of averaging $0.5x^2 + 1$ from $3$ to $8$:

$$
\text{average} = \frac{\displaystyle\int_3^8 (0.5x^2 + 1)\,dx}{8 - 3} = \frac{126.25}{5} = 25.25
$$

And there's our answer!
