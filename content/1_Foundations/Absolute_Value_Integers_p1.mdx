# Absolute Value and Integer Operations

## Integer Operations

Integers are the positive and negative whole numbers, including zero:

$$
\{\dots,-3,-2,-1,0,1,2,3,\dots\}
$$

### Addition and Subtraction

- Same signs: add and keep the sign.
- Different signs: subtract and keep the sign of the larger absolute value.

For example:

$$
-7 + 3 = -4
$$


### Multiplication and Division

- Same signs give a positive result.
- Different signs give a negative result.

For example:

$$
(-4)(-5)=20
$$

$$
(-4)(5)=-20
$$


## Absolute Value

The absolute value of a number is its distance from $0$ on the number line.

Absolute value is written using vertical bars:

$$
|x|
$$

For example:

$$
|5| = 5
$$

and:

$$
|-5| = 5
$$

because both $5$ and $-5$ are $5$ units away from $0$.

In general:

$$
|x|=
\begin{cases}
x, & x \ge 0 \\
-x, & x < 0
\end{cases}
$$

## Properties of Absolute Value

### Product Rule

$$
|ab| = |a||b|
$$

Example:

$$
|-3 \cdot 4| = |-12| = 12
$$

and:

$$
|-3||4| = 3 \cdot 4 = 12
$$

### Quotient Rule

$$
\left|\frac{a}{b}\right|
=
\frac{|a|}{|b|}
\quad (b \ne 0)
$$

### Triangle Inequality

$$
|a+b| \le |a| + |b|
$$

This means the distance traveled together cannot exceed the sum of the individual distances.

> ### Example 1: 
>Find all solutions to the equation |x ^ 2 - 3x| = 4 .
Solution: We can split the problem into two cases: either 
$$ 
x ^ 2 - 3x = 4 \\
\text{ or } \\
x ^ 2 - 3x = - 4 
$$
The first case yields the solutions 4 and -1; the second yields $ \frac{3 \pm i \sqrt{7}}{2} $ If we restrict ourselves to real solutions, only 4 and -1 are acceptable.

> ### Example 2: Solve the equation |(x + 2) / (3x - 1)| = 5
This gives two cases:

$$
\frac{x+2}{3x-1}=5
\quad \text{or} \quad
\frac{x+2}{3x-1}=-5
$$

For the first case:

$$
x+2=5(3x-1)
$$

$$
x+2=15x-5
$$

$$
7=14x
$$

$$
x=\frac12
$$

For the second case:

$$
x+2=-5(3x-1)
$$

$$
x+2=-15x+5
$$

$$
16x=3
$$

$$
x=\frac{3}{16}
$$

Since:

$$
3x-1 \ne 0
$$

we must have:

$$
x \ne \frac13
$$

Both solutions are valid.

Thus, the solutions are:

$$
\boxed{x=\frac12 \text{ or } x=\frac{3}{16}}
$$

## Sigma Notation

Sigma notation represents sums.

The Greek letter sigma is written as:

$$
\sum
$$

For example:

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{5} k
$$

means:

$$
1+2+3+4+5
$$

which equals:

$$
15
$$

### Example

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{4} n^2
$$

means:

$$
1^2+2^2+3^2+4^2
$$

which equals:

$$
30
$$

## Important Summation Formulae

### Sum of the First \(n\) Natural Numbers

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n} k
=
\frac{n(n+1)}{2}
$$

<details>
<summary><strong>Proof</strong></summary>

Let:

$$
S = 1+2+3+\dots+n
$$

Write the sum in reverse order:

$$
S = n+(n-1)+(n-2)+\dots+1
$$

Adding the two equations term by term:

$$
2S=(n+1)+(n+1)+\dots+(n+1)
$$

There are \(n\) terms, so:

$$
2S=n(n+1)
$$

Thus:

$$
S=\frac{n(n+1)}{2}
$$

</details>

### Sum of the Squares of the First \(n\) Natural Numbers

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2
=
\frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}
$$

<details>
<summary><strong>Proof Sketch</strong></summary>

One way to prove this formula is by mathematical induction.

Assume:

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2
=
\frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}
$$

Then:

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n+1} k^2
=
\frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}+(n+1)^2
$$

Factoring and simplifying gives:

$$
\frac{(n+1)(n+2)(2n+3)}{6}
$$

which matches the formula with \(n+1\).

Thus, the formula holds for all positive integers \(n\).

</details>

### Sum of the Cubes of the First \(n\) Natural Numbers

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^3
=
\left(
\frac{n(n+1)}{2}
\right)^2
$$

<details>
<summary><strong>Interesting Observation</strong></summary>

The sum of cubes equals the square of the sum of the first \(n\) natural numbers.

That is:

$$
1^3+2^3+\dots+n^3
=
(1+2+\dots+n)^2
$$

For example:

$$
1^3+2^3+3^3
=
1+8+27
=
36
$$

and:

$$
(1+2+3)^2
=
6^2
=
36
$$

This identity can also be proved using induction.

</details>

## Pi Notation

Pi notation represents products.

The Greek capital pi is written as:

$$
\prod
$$

For example:

$$
\prod_{k=1}^{4} k
$$

means:

$$
1 \cdot 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 4
$$

which equals:

$$
24
$$

Another example:

$$
\prod_{n=2}^{5} n
$$

means:

$$
2 \cdot 3 \cdot 4 \cdot 5
$$

which equals:

$$
120
$$
