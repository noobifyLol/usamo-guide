## Overview

Linear equations ($ax + b = c$) and linear inequalities ($ax + b < c$) form the
backbone of AMC 8 algebra. This module covers solving techniques, handling
fractions and decimals in equations, and the key rule that flipping an inequality
sign is required when multiplying or dividing by a negative number.

## Key Ideas

- Isolate the variable: combine like terms, then undo addition/subtraction, then
  undo multiplication/division.
- When you multiply or divide both sides of an inequality by a negative number,
  flip the inequality sign.
- Check solutions by substituting back into the original equation.
- Compound inequalities like $2 < x + 1 < 7$ can be solved by subtracting 1
  from all three parts.

## Core Skills

### One-Step and Two-Step Equations

Undo operations in reverse order of operations.

### Equations with Fractions

Clear denominators by multiplying both sides by the LCD.

### Compound Inequalities

Apply the same operation to all three parts of the inequality.

### Inequality Graphing

Represent solution sets on a number line using open/closed circles.

## Worked Example

Solve $3x - 7 \le 2x + 5$.

Subtract $2x$: $x - 7 \le 5$. Add 7: $x \le 12$.

## More Examples

## Example 1: Variables on Both Sides

Solve 5x+8=2x+20.

Move variables to one side: 3x+8=20. Then 3x=12, so x=4.

## Example 2: Fraction Equation

Solve $\dfrac{x}{3} + \dfrac{1}{2} = 2$.

Multiply everything by $6$: $2x + 3 = 12$. Thus $2x = 9$, giving $x = \dfrac{9}{2}$.

## Example 3: Flipping the Inequality

Solve −4x+1>13.

First, subtract 1: −4x>12. 

Then, divide by −4, flipping the sign: x<−3.

NOTE: Solutions from here on out are not step by step solutions, but merely solutions that provide the key ideas and insights, so that you can fill in the gaps (to make you think!!).

## Example 4: Decimal Trap

Solve 0.2x+1.5=3.9.

Multiply by 10 to clear decimals: 2x+15=39, so 2x=24 and x=12.

## Example 5: Compound Inequality

Solve 2<3x−4≤11.

Add 4 to all three parts: 
6<3x≤15. 

Divide by 3: 2<x≤5.

## Example 6: Math Competition Style Word Equation

A number is multiplied by 4, then 7 is subtracted to get 29. Find the number.
Translate directly into an equation: 4x−7=29. Solving gives 4x=36, so x=9.

## Strategy Checklist

Isolate the variable systematically.

Clear fractions or decimals early if possible.

Flip the inequality sign when dividing or multiplying by a negative.

Check solutions by substituting back into the original equation.

For compound inequalities, perform operations on all three parts.

## Common Mistakes

Forgetting to distribute negatives correctly.

Forgetting to flip the inequality sign after dividing by a negative.

Making arithmetic mistakes while clearing fractions.

Applying operations to only part of a compound inequality.
