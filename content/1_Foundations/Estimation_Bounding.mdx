## Overview

Estimation is a powerful meta-skill on the AMC 8. When you can quickly bound an
answer between two nice numbers, you can eliminate three or four answer choices
before doing any precise computation. This module trains that instinct.

## Key Ideas

- Round factors to one significant figure before multiplying to get a quick
  estimate.
- Use upper and lower bounds: if $a < x < b$, then $f(a) < f(x) < f(b)$ for
  increasing functions.
- On multiple-choice tests, estimate first, then only compute precisely if two
  choices are close together.
- Squeezing: find a simple number above and below the true answer.

## Core Skills

### Rough Multiplication

Replace $197 \times 43$ with $200 \times 40 = 8000$ to narrow the answer range.

### Eliminating Answer Choices

If you estimate 8000 and the choices are 3000, 5000, 8451, 12000, 20000 — only
8451 is close.

### Bounding Sums

For large sums, compare to a known formula or integral bound.

## Worked Example

Estimate $\frac{2021 \times 49}{98}$ without a calculator.

$\frac{2021 \times 49}{98} = 2021 \times \frac{1}{2} \approx 1010.5.$

The exact value is $1010.5$.
