## Triple angle Identities

Triple angle identities express trigonometric functions of $3x$ in terms of
functions of $x$.

They are useful when a problem contains angles such as $3x$, $60^\circ-3x$,
or when equations involve cubic expressions in sine or cosine.

### Sine Triple angle Formula

Start with

$$
\sin 3x=\sin(2x+x).
$$

Using the angle addition formula,

$$
\sin 3x=\sin 2x\cos x+\cos 2x\sin x.
$$

Now substitute

$$
\sin 2x=2\sin x\cos x
$$

and

$$
\cos 2x=1-2\sin^2 x.
$$

This gives

$$
\sin 3x
=
2\sin x\cos^2 x+\sin x(1-2\sin^2 x).
$$

Since $\cos^2 x=1-\sin^2 x$,

$$
\sin 3x
=
2\sin x(1-\sin^2 x)+\sin x(1-2\sin^2 x).
$$

Simplifying,

$$
\sin 3x=3\sin x-4\sin^3 x.
$$

### Cosine Triple angle Formula

Similarly,

$$
\cos 3x=\cos(2x+x).
$$

Using the angle addition formula,

$$
\cos 3x=\cos 2x\cos x-\sin 2x\sin x.
$$

Substitute

$$
\cos 2x=2\cos^2 x-1
$$

and

$$
\sin 2x=2\sin x\cos x.
$$

Then

$$
\cos 3x
=
(2\cos^2 x-1)\cos x-2\sin^2 x\cos x.
$$

Using $\sin^2 x=1-\cos^2 x$,

$$
\cos 3x
=
2\cos^3 x-\cos x-2(1-\cos^2 x)\cos x.
$$

Simplifying,

$$
\cos 3x=4\cos^3 x-3\cos x.
$$

### Tangent Triple angle Formula

Using the tangent addition formula, you get

$$
\tan 3x
=
\frac{3\tan x-\tan^3 x}{1-3\tan^2 x},
$$

provided the expressions are defined.

This formula is less commonly memorised, but it can be useful in certain
equation solving problems.

## Sums of Trigonometric Series

Some problems ask us to evaluate sums such as

$$
\sin x+\sin 2x+\sin 3x+\cdots+\sin nx
$$

or

$$
\cos x+\cos 2x+\cos 3x+\cdots+\cos nx.
$$

There are several ways to approach these sums. A useful method is to multiply
by a carefully chosen trigonometric factor so that the sum telescopes.

## Sum of Sines

Let

$$
S=\sin x+\sin 2x+\cdots+\sin nx.
$$

Multiply both sides by $2\sin\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)$.

Using the identity

$$
2\sin A\sin B=\cos(A-B)-\cos(A+B),
$$

we get

$$
2\sin\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)S
=
\sum_{k=1}^{n}
2\sin\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)\sin(kx).
$$

Now apply product to sum:

$$
2\sin\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)\sin(kx)
=
\cos\left(kx-\frac{x}{2}\right)
-
\cos\left(kx+\frac{x}{2}\right).
$$

So the sum becomes

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n}
\left[
\cos\left(\left(k-\frac{1}{2}\right)x\right)
-
\cos\left(\left(k+\frac{1}{2}\right)x\right)
\right].
$$

Most terms cancel. We are left with

$$
2\sin\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)S
=
\cos\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)
-
\cos\left(\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right)x\right).
$$

Using sum to product,

$$
\cos\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)
-
\cos\left(\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right)x\right)
=
2\sin\left(\frac{(n+1)x}{2}\right)
\sin\left(\frac{nx}{2}\right).
$$

Therefore,

$$
\sin x+\sin 2x+\cdots+\sin nx
=
\frac{
\sin\left(\frac{nx}{2}\right)
\sin\left(\frac{(n+1)x}{2}\right)
}{
\sin\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)
}.
$$

This formula is valid when $\sin\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)\neq 0$. Special cases
where the denominator is zero should be handled separately.

## Sum of Cosines

A similar telescoping argument gives

$$
\cos x+\cos 2x+\cdots+\cos nx
=
\frac{
\sin\left(\frac{nx}{2}\right)
\cos\left(\frac{(n+1)x}{2}\right)
}{
\sin\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)
},
$$

again assuming $\sin\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)\neq 0$.

These two formulas are especially useful when evaluating long trigonometric
sums.

## Worked Examples

### Example 1

> Simplify $\sin 5x\cos 2x$.

Use the product to sum identity

$$
\sin A\cos B
=
\frac{1}{2}(\sin(A+B)+\sin(A-B)).
$$

With $A=5x$ and $B=2x$,

$$
\sin 5x\cos 2x
=
\frac{1}{2}(\sin 7x+\sin 3x).
$$

### Example 2

> Express $\sin^2 x$ in terms of $\cos 2x$.

From the double angle identity,

$$
\cos 2x=1-2\sin^2 x.
$$

Rearranging,

$$
\sin^2 x=\frac{1-\cos 2x}{2}.
$$

This identity is useful when reducing powers of trigonometric functions.

### Example 3

> Solve $4\cos^3 x - 3\cos x = \frac{1}{2}$ for $0^\circ \le x < 360^\circ$.

Recognise the left hand side as the cosine triple angle formula:

$$
4\cos^3 x - 3\cos x = \cos 3x.
$$

So the equation becomes

$$
\cos 3x = \frac{1}{2}.
$$

We know that $\cos \theta = \frac{1}{2}$ when $\theta = 60^\circ + 360^\circ k$
or $\theta = 300^\circ + 360^\circ k$.

So

$$
3x = 60^\circ + 360^\circ k
\quad\text{or}\quad
3x = 300^\circ + 360^\circ k.
$$

From the first family: $x = 20^\circ, 140^\circ, 260^\circ$.

From the second family: $x = 100^\circ, 220^\circ, 340^\circ$.

The six solutions in $[0^\circ, 360^\circ)$ are

$$
x = 20^\circ, 100^\circ, 140^\circ, 220^\circ, 260^\circ, 340^\circ.
$$

### Example 4

> Evaluate
>
> $$
> \sin 10^\circ+\sin 20^\circ+\cdots+\sin 80^\circ.
> $$

This is a sine series with first angle $10^\circ$, common step $10^\circ$, and
last angle $80^\circ$.

Using the sum formula with $x=10^\circ$ and $n=8$,

$$
\sin 10^\circ+\sin 20^\circ+\cdots+\sin 80^\circ
=
\frac{
\sin 40^\circ\sin 45^\circ
}{
\sin 5^\circ
}.
$$

This is already exact form.

### Example 5

> Show that
>
> $$
> \sin x+\sin 3x+\sin 5x+\cdots+\sin(2n-1)x
> =
> \frac{\sin^2(nx)}{\sin x}.
> $$

Let

$$
S=\sin x+\sin 3x+\cdots+\sin(2n-1)x.
$$

Multiply by $2\sin x$:

$$
2\sin x S
=
\sum_{k=1}^{n}2\sin x\sin((2k-1)x).
$$

Using

$$
2\sin A\sin B=\cos(A-B)-\cos(A+B),
$$

we get

$$
2\sin x\sin((2k-1)x)
=
\cos((2k-2)x)-\cos(2kx).
$$

Therefore the sum telescopes:

$$
2\sin xS
=
(1-\cos 2x)+(\cos 2x-\cos 4x)+\cdots+
(\cos(2n-2)x-\cos 2nx).
$$

All middle terms cancel, leaving

$$
2\sin xS=1-\cos 2nx.
$$

Since

$$
1-\cos 2nx=2\sin^2(nx),
$$

we obtain

$$
S=
\frac{\sin^2(nx)}{\sin x}.
$$

## Key Contest Insight

Most trigonometric problems become easier after changing the form of the
expression.

Use:

- Product to sum when products appear
- Sum to product when sums need to be factored
- Double angle identities when squares appear
- Auxiliary angle when an expression has the form $a\sin x+b\cos x$
- Triple angle identities when cubes or $3x$ appear
- Telescoping when a long trigonometric series appears


The main challenge is recognising which identity makes the expression simpler.

## Strategy Tips

- Do not expand blindly. Look for structure first.
- Products such as $\sin A\cos B$ often suggest product to sum.
- Squares such as $\sin^2 x$ and $\cos^2 x$ often suggest double angle
  identities.
- For expressions like $a\sin x+b\cos x$, use the auxiliary angle method to
  find maximum and minimum values quickly.
- Cubes such as $\sin^3 x$ and $\cos^3 x$ often suggest triple angle identities.
- Long sums with evenly spaced angles often telescope after multiplying by a
  suitable sine factor.
- Always check whether special cases make denominators zero.
- In degree notation, keep track of whether angles are in degrees or radians.


## Common Pitfalls

- Mixing degrees and radians.
- Forgetting the negative sign in

$$
\cos X-\cos Y
=
-2\sin\left(\frac{X+Y}{2}\right)
\sin\left(\frac{X-Y}{2}\right).
$$

- Using a formula without checking whether division by zero occurs.
- Applying a product to sum identity with the wrong signs.
- Forgetting that triple angle identities contain cubic terms.
- Expanding trigonometric expressions when a direct identity would simplify
  them.
